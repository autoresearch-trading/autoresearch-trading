# Council-6 Review (DL Architect / SSL) — Pre-Publication Strengthening

**Date:** 2026-04-26 (PM, post Gate 4 PASS, post multi-probe drop)
**Subject:** Whether `step4-program-end-state.md` is publishable as written or needs SSL-quality probe strengthening
**Verdict:** **Publish substantially as-is, with two zero-cost reframings and ONE additional non-amendment-budget diagnostic (RankMe).** Reject the larger probe battery.

## Q1 — Is the SSL framing right?

**The framing is correct but mislocated.** "+1pp linearly-extractable direction signal at H500, temporally stable but not amplifiable by fine-tuning" is an honest description of what the *probe* found. It is NOT a description of what the *encoder* learned. The encoder produces 256-d embeddings with effective rank ~215, embed std 0.769, CKA-stable across seeds (per Gate 1 diagnostics), and per-symbol-clustered (cohesion delta +0.037, symbol-ID 0.934). That is a well-conditioned, non-collapsed, per-symbol-organized representation — full stop. Direction is one downstream slice. The current writeup fuses these into a single direction-centric headline, which understates what was measured. Standard SSL-quality metrics (alignment-uniformity, RankMe, linear-eval transfer) would let us report representation geometry independent of the direction-prediction frame; the absence of these in the writeup is a publication weakness, not a measurement gap.

## Q2 — Pre-publication probes worth adding

Of the five proposed, only **RankMe (Garrido et al. 2023)** clears the bar: it requires no labels, no amendment-budget consumption (it is a property of the embedding matrix on held-out data, not a hypothesis test against any pre-registered threshold), runs in minutes on the existing checkpoint, and produces a single normalized-effective-rank scalar that directly characterizes how much of the 256-d space the encoder uses on Feb+Mar. Report it next to embed_std and CKA-drift in the diagnostics section. **CKA-drift to the failed Phase B checkpoint** is also free — we already cite 0.061 in passing; formalize it as a reported scalar, not a parenthetical.

The other three are more dangerous than helpful at this stage. **Alignment-uniformity** requires defining "positive pairs" on held-out data — overlapping windows from same symbol-day is one choice, but defending it as the canonical operationalization will eat amendment budget the same way C1 calibration did. **Symbol-ID re-measurement on Feb+Mar** is fine to add, but it tests a hypothesis we have already answered (per-symbol geometry is real, +0.037 cohesion delta is decisive); the held-out re-measurement would add noise, not information, unless the result inverts — which it won't. **Linear eval to a non-direction continuous target** (mean effort_vs_result, mean climax_score) is council-4's path C-narrow in disguise; the multi-probe-battery calibration disaster from this morning is a clear warning that defining a non-direction downstream task on the held-out distribution is exactly the move that consumed our amendment budget once already. Do not retry it under a new name.

## Q3 — Should cluster cohesion be the headline?

**Yes — co-headline with the +1pp signal, not buried in diagnostics.** The current Section "Key diagnostic observations" buries the most causally-explanatory finding of the program three paragraphs deep. The per-symbol-not-universal representation geometry (cluster cohesion delta +0.037 vs +0.10 universality threshold; symbol-ID 0.934) is what *causes* Gate 2's regression-to-0.50 pattern: the encoder learned 25 symbol-specific tape geometries that don't share a representation axis, so fine-tuning at lr=5e-5 has no shared trunk to specialize and instead consumes the +1pp margin per-symbol via day-conditional shortcuts. The Gate 2 failure mechanism only makes sense once the per-symbol geometry is established; reporting them in the opposite order (signal first, geometry as diagnostic) inverts the causal story. **Recommended reframe:** lead paragraph reports both findings as conjoined — "the encoder produced a per-symbol-clustered 256-d representation (symbol-ID 0.934, cohesion delta +0.037) within which a linearly-extractable +1pp directional signal at H500 is temporally stable but not amplifiable by supervised fine-tuning." This is one sentence, no amendment, cleaner causal logic.

## Q4 — Publish-as-is verdict

**Publish substantially as-is, with three zero-cost edits:** (a) add RankMe to the diagnostics block (no threshold, no hypothesis test — published as a representation-quality scalar alongside embed_std and effective_rank); (b) elevate cluster cohesion + symbol-ID to a co-headline with the +1pp signal per Q3 reframe; (c) formalize the CKA-drift 0.061 as a named reported quantity, not a parenthetical. None of these consume amendment budget — they are descriptive statistics on the existing checkpoint, not new hypothesis tests against pre-registered thresholds. Any larger probe battery (alignment-uniformity, linear-eval-to-continuous-target, held-out symbol-ID re-measurement) should be rejected: the marginal information added is small, the falsifiability cost compounds with the multi-probe-battery drop already on the books, and the program's binding pre-registration has already reached its end-state per council-5's ratification.

**Honest read:** the writeup is publishable now. The DSR effective N=3 disclosure is sufficient. The +1pp finding is small and the per-symbol geometry finding is more important than the writeup currently signals — but those are presentation issues, not measurement gaps.
