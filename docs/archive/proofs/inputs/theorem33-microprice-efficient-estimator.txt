# Theorem 33: Microprice as Optimal Efficient Price Estimator

Based on Gatheral & Oomen (2010), "Zero-intelligence realized variance estimation."

## Definitions

Consider a limit order book with best bid price B, best ask price A, best bid quantity q_B, best ask quantity q_A.

Define:
- Midprice: M = (A + B) / 2
- Spread: S = A - B > 0
- Microprice: P_micro = (B * q_A + A * q_B) / (q_B + q_A)

Let the true efficient price be P* (unobservable), satisfying E[P*] = E[next trade price | current book state].

Define the imbalance ratio: I = q_B / (q_B + q_A), so I ∈ (0, 1).

## Claims

1. **Microprice as weighted midprice.** Prove that:

     P_micro = M + (I - 1/2) * S

   That is, the microprice equals the midprice plus an adjustment proportional to the imbalance and the spread. When q_B = q_A (balanced book), I = 1/2 and P_micro = M. When q_B > q_A (more bids), I > 1/2 and P_micro > M (price is pushed up).

2. **Microprice deviation identity.** The deviation from midprice is:

     P_micro - M = (q_B - q_A) / (2 * (q_B + q_A)) * S = (2*I - 1) * S / 2

   This is exactly our feature `microprice_dev`. Prove this is equivalent to half the spread times the normalized imbalance.

3. **Optimality under uniform fill probability.** Under the "zero-intelligence" model where the next trade hits the ask with probability q_B / (q_B + q_A) and hits the bid with probability q_A / (q_B + q_A) (larger bid queue means more likely to trade at the ask):

     E[next_trade_price | book] = A * q_B/(q_B + q_A) + B * q_A/(q_B + q_A) = P_micro

   Prove that the microprice is the minimum-variance unbiased estimator of the next trade price under this model.

4. **Microprice strictly dominates midprice as estimator.** Define:
   - MSE_mid = E[(M - P*)^2]
   - MSE_micro = E[(P_micro - P*)^2]

   Under the zero-intelligence model where P* = P_micro:

     MSE_micro = 0
     MSE_mid = ((I - 1/2) * S)^2 = (microprice_dev)^2

   So MSE_mid >= MSE_micro with equality iff I = 1/2 (balanced book). Prove this.

5. **Information content of microprice_dev.** The squared microprice deviation E[(P_micro - M)^2] = Var(I) * S^2 / 4. This variance is maximized when I is uniformly distributed (maximum uncertainty about imbalance) and zero when I is constant (always balanced). Prove that microprice_dev has higher variance (and thus more potential predictive power) when:
   - Spread is wider (more room for the microprice to deviate)
   - Imbalance is more variable (more directional pressure changes)
