## T42: Funding Rate P&L Impact

### Claim

For a position with direction d ∈ {+1 (long), -1 (short)}, funding rate r_f per settlement period, held for T settlement periods:

```
funding_pnl = -d × r_f × T
```

Longs (d=+1) pay when r_f > 0. Shorts (d=-1) pay when r_f < 0.

### Setup

- Pacifica uses hourly funding settlement (like Drift Protocol)
- Formula: `funding_rate_hourly = (1/24) × (market_twap - oracle_twap) / oracle_twap`
- Our steps ≈ 100 trades ≈ 1-2 seconds per step (BTC)
- 1 hour ≈ 1,800-3,600 steps

### Proof

**Step 1:** Per settlement, the funding payment is `r_f` applied to notional. For a unit position: `payment = d × r_f`.

**Step 2:** Over T consecutive settlements, total funding P&L:
```
funding_pnl = Σ_{t=1}^{T} (-d × r_f(t))
```
For constant funding rate: `funding_pnl = -d × r_f × T`.

**Step 3:** Condition for funding drag to exceed per-trade alpha α:
```
|E[r_f]| × T̄_hours > α
```
where T̄_hours is the average hold duration in hours.

### External Research (Exa)

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Baseline funding rate | 0.125 bps/hour (0.01%/8h) | Industry standard |
| Positive funding frequency | 74-92% of time | CryptoQuant (9yr), BitMEX Q3 2025 |
| Median daily aggregate | +0.485%/day | CryptoQuant, n=3,572 days |
| Drift hourly clamp | 12.5 bps/hour (top tier) | Drift docs |
| Distribution shape | Positively skewed, leptokurtic, fat-tailed | All sources |
| Autocorrelation (lag 1) | 0.966-0.998 | Zhivkov 2026 |
| 2025 compressed yield | ~4% APY (down from ~11%) | BitMEX 2025 |

### Empirical Verification (Our Data — 8 symbols, 5 sampled dates)

| Symbol | Median (bps/hr) | % Positive | P5 (bps) | P95 (bps) |
|--------|----------------|-----------|---------|----------|
| BTC | 0.12 | 91.0% | -0.02 | 0.12 |
| ETH | 0.12 | 75.8% | -0.12 | 0.15 |
| SOL | 0.02 | 56.7% | -0.32 | 0.15 |
| DOGE | 0.12 | 85.9% | -0.07 | 0.15 |
| HYPE | 0.12 | 92.9% | -0.05 | 0.15 |
| FARTCOIN | 0.12 | 97.5% | 0.12 | 0.15 |
| AAVE | 0.06 | 67.7% | -0.16 | 0.13 |
| LTC | 0.04 | 57.2% | -0.25 | 0.12 |
| **AGGREGATE** | **0.12** | **78.1%** | — | — |

Our empirical median (0.12 bps/hr) matches the external research baseline (0.125 bps/hr) almost exactly.

### Materiality Assessment

At our hold duration of MIN_HOLD=1200 steps ≈ 20-40 minutes:
- Funding settlement: ~0.3-0.7 settlements per trade (hourly settlement)
- Expected funding cost per trade: 0.12 bps × 0.5 ≈ **0.06 bps**
- Our fee barriers: 80 bps (fee_mult=8.0)
- **Funding/Fee ratio: 0.0016 — funding is 0.16% of the fee barrier — negligible**

At MIN_HOLD=100 steps ≈ 2-3 minutes: even more negligible (~0.02 bps/trade).

### Edge Cases

1. **Extreme funding events** (COVID crash: -25.7%/day): Funding becomes material during stress, but positions would likely be stopped out by price movement first.
2. **Persistent position** (hours to days): Funding compounds. A position held 24 hours at 0.125 bps/hour = 3 bps — still small vs 80 bps barriers.
3. **Asymmetric impact**: Funding is positive ~74-92% of time, systematically penalizing longs. Our model trades both directions, partially self-hedging.

### Implementation

- `funding_rates` computed on the fly in `make_env()` from cached timestamps + raw funding Parquet
- Charged per step when holding: longs pay positive funding, shorts receive it
- Backwards-compatible: `funding_rates=None` means no charge
- 6 tests in `tests/test_funding_cost.py`, all passing

## T43: Conditional Slippage During Signal Periods

### Claim

When the model generates a trade signal, it coincides with elevated market activity. The effective spread during signal periods is:

```
E[spread | signal] = R × E[spread]
```

where R = E[spread | high activity] / E[spread] > 1.

If R > 1, our static slippage model underestimates true execution cost by factor R.

### Setup

Current slippage model in `TradingEnv.step()`:
```python
slippage_bps = half_spread + 3.0  # MEV + market impact buffer
```

This uses the unconditional spread, not the spread conditioned on signal timing.

### Proof

**Step 1:** By Glosten-Milgrom (1985), the spread compensates market makers for adverse selection:
```
S = 2α(V_H - V_L)
```
where α = probability of informed trading. During high activity, α increases.

**Step 2:** From spread decomposition (Huang & Stoll, 1997):
```
Spread = Order_Processing + Inventory_Risk + Adverse_Selection
```
During high activity, all three components increase:
- Inventory risk rises (volatility up)
- Adverse selection rises (informed flow surges)
- Order processing unchanged

**Step 3:** The conditional spread follows a power law with volatility:
```
E[spread | σ] / E[spread | σ₀] ≈ (σ / σ₀)^0.7
```
The exponent 0.7 is empirically calibrated between the square-root law (0.5) and linear (1.0), grounded in Amberdata and Talos data showing crypto spreads widen superlinearly.

### External Research

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Moderate volatility (2x normal) | 1.5-2.0x spread | Amberdata, Kaiko |
| High volatility (3-5x normal) | 2.0-3.0x spread | Amberdata, academic lit |
| Extreme stress (>5x vol) | 5-10x spread | Amberdata Aug 2024 |
| Almgren-Chriss crypto calibration | Power-law impact | Ramirez & Sanchez Lopez 2023 |
| Talos market impact model | `c1×S + σ√T × f(π)` | Hoch 2025 |
| Spread dominance in microstructure | Confirmed via SHAP | Bieganowski & Slepaczuk 2026 |

### Empirical Verification (Our Data — 23 symbols, test set)

**Result: R = 1.00x (no conditional widening detected)**

All 23 symbols show E[spread|high activity] ≈ E[spread]. The ratio ranges from 0.96x (ENA) to 1.02x (SUI), with mean and median both at 1.00x.

**Interpretation:** At our resolution (100-trade batches, ~1-2 seconds per step), spreads do not widen during high-activity periods on Pacifica. This could be because:
1. Pacifica market makers maintain spreads through activity spikes at this timescale
2. Our activity proxy (trade_arrival_rate percentile) doesn't capture the regime shifts that cause widening
3. Spread widening happens at extreme stress events (crashes), not at the p75 activity level we measure
4. The external research (1.5-2.0x) was based on daily/hourly data, not sub-second data

### Materiality Assessment

**R = 1.00 means our static slippage model is correct for normal conditions.**

No code change needed. The current `slippage = half_spread + 3 bps` is accurate.

**Caveat:** This does NOT rule out spread widening during extreme stress events (external research shows 5-10x during crashes). Our test set may not contain such events. The 3 bps impact buffer provides some margin.

### Edge Cases

1. **Oracle-based DEXs** (Jupiter Perps): Zero price impact by design — trades execute at oracle price. Conditional slippage is less relevant.
2. **Orderbook-based DEXs** (Drift, Pacifica): Conditional slippage is real and material.
3. **Post-event hangover**: Spreads remain elevated 1-3 days after stress (1.3-1.5x), affecting subsequent signals.

### Implementation

- **Status: ANALYSIS PHASE** — conditional spread ratio being measured from our data
- If R > 1.3 from our data, will implement conditional multiplier in `TradingEnv.step()`
- If R < 1.3, document as known underestimate but accept (within noise)

## T44: Correlated Drawdown and Effective Diversification

### Claim

For a portfolio of N symbols with uniform pairwise correlation ρ, the portfolio drawdown relative to a single-symbol drawdown is:

```
DD_portfolio ≈ DD_single × √(1 + (N-1)ρ) / √N
```

The effective number of independent bets:
```
N_eff = N / (1 + (N-1)ρ)
```

### Setup

- N = 23 passing symbols (25 minus CRV, XPL excluded)
- Portfolio Sortino = mean per-symbol Sortino (implicitly assumes low correlation)
- Max DD guardrail: 20% per symbol

### Proof

**Step 1:** For N assets with equal weight w = 1/N and common variance σ² and pairwise correlation ρ, portfolio variance:
```
σ²_p = w²Nσ² + w²N(N-1)ρσ² = σ²/N × [1 + (N-1)ρ]
```

**Step 2:** Portfolio volatility relative to individual:
```
σ_p / σ = √(1 + (N-1)ρ) / √N
```

**Step 3:** Applying this to drawdown (assuming drawdown scales with volatility):
```
DD_portfolio ≈ DD_single × √(1 + (N-1)ρ) / √N
```

**Step 4:** For our metrics, the diversification multiplier on Sortino is:
```
Sortino_portfolio = Sortino_single × √(N / (1 + (N-1)ρ)) = Sortino_single × √N_eff
```

### External Research

| Parameter | Normal Period | Stress Period | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|--------|
| Avg pairwise ρ (crypto) | 0.50-0.60 | 0.70-0.85 | Lahajnar & Rozanec 2020, The Tie 2021 |
| First PC variance explained | 40-60% | 70-80%+ | Kwapien et al. 2021, Marti 2022 |
| Distribution of ρ | All positive, unimodal | Compressed toward 1 | The Tie (5,578 pairs) |
| Mean-variance underestimate | — | 40-60% tail risk miss | Ang & Bekaert 2002 |

### Computed Values

| Regime | ρ | N_eff (N=23) | √N_eff | DD multiplier | Portfolio DD (if single=20%) |
|--------|---|-------------|---------|--------------|-----|
| Independence | 0.00 | 23.0 | 4.80 | 0.21x | 4.2% |
| **Normal (typical)** | **0.55** | **1.76** | **1.33** | **0.75x** | **15.1%** |
| Moderate stress | 0.70 | 1.40 | 1.18 | 0.85x | 16.9% |
| Severe stress | 0.80 | 1.24 | 1.11 | 0.90x | 18.0% |
| Perfect correlation | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00x | 20.0% |

### Empirical Verification (Our Data — 23 symbols, test set, 100-trade-batch returns)

| Period | Avg Pairwise ρ | N_eff | DD Multiplier | Portfolio DD (if single=20%) |
|--------|---------------|-------|--------------|-----|
| **Full period** | **0.282** | **3.2** | **0.56x** | **11.2%** |
| **BTC worst 5%** | **0.198** | **4.3** | **0.48x** | **9.6%** |

**Key finding:** Our empirical correlation (ρ=0.28) is MUCH lower than external research (ρ=0.50-0.60). This is because:
1. We measure 100-trade batch returns (~1-2 seconds), not daily returns
2. At microstructure timescales, idiosyncratic noise dominates
3. The "correlation goes to 1" effect is a daily/weekly phenomenon

**Stress correlation decreases (0.198 vs 0.282)**: Counter-intuitive but makes sense at our timescale — during BTC crashes, altcoin microstructure becomes MORE idiosyncratic (wider spreads, irregular fills, different maker behavior), reducing tick-level correlation even as daily returns converge.

### Materiality Assessment

**Our portfolio is better diversified than expected** at the timescale we trade:
- N_eff = 3.2 (not 1.8 as external research suggested)
- Portfolio DD ≈ 11% vs 20% per-symbol guardrail
- Diversification multiplier on Sortino: √3.2 = 1.79x

**However**, this is tick-level diversification, not portfolio-level risk diversification. For risk management (drawdown guardrails, position sizing), the daily correlation structure (ρ=0.50-0.60) is more relevant.

### Edge Cases

1. **Non-uniform correlation**: BTC-ETH have higher correlation (~0.80) than BTC-DOGE (~0.40). The uniform ρ is an approximation.
2. **Altcoin universe**: Smaller-cap perps may have lower baseline correlation but higher stress correlation (liquidity-driven).
3. **Same-DEX risk**: All 25 symbols trade on Pacifica, adding shared platform risk not captured by price correlation.

### Implementation

- **Status: ANALYSIS PHASE** — measuring empirical correlation from our data
- This is primarily an interpretation/guardrail adjustment, not a code change
- May adjust portfolio DD guardrail from 20% to 15% based on findings

## T45: Execution Latency

### Claim

For Solana DEX perpetual futures, execution latency does not need to be modeled as a separate cost because:

1. Latency (~400ms) is a fraction of one step (~1-2 seconds)
2. Signal horizon (trade_batch=100 trades) far exceeds latency
3. Spread model already captures the relevant price uncertainty during execution

### External Research

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Solana slot time | 400ms | Protocol specification |
| Co-located fill time | 420-450ms | RPC Fast benchmarks 2026 |
| Non-optimized fill time | 500-800ms | Carbium benchmarks 2026 |
| P99 tail latency (shared RPC) | 800-3000ms | Carbium 2026 |
| MEV risk for perps | **Negligible** | IMC 2025 (sandwich targets AMM swaps, not perps) |
| Priority fees | $0.002-0.005/tx | Negligible vs 5-10 bps fees |
| Upcoming: Alpenglow finality | ~150ms (Q3 2026) | Solana roadmap |

### Assessment

| Scenario | Latency | Model Impact |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Oracle-based perps (Jupiter) | ~400ms (1 slot) | No price impact, oracle price |
| Orderbook perps (Drift/Pacifica) | 400-800ms | Already captured by spread model |
| Signal decay during latency | 400ms / 1000ms per step ≈ 0.4 steps | Negligible signal decay |

### Conclusion

**No implementation needed.** The 400ms assumption is reasonable, and latency costs are already implicitly modeled through:
- Protocol fees (5-10 bps)
- Spread/slippage model (half_spread + 3 bps impact buffer)
- The 3 bps impact buffer was specifically designed to capture MEV + market impact, which subsumes latency costs
