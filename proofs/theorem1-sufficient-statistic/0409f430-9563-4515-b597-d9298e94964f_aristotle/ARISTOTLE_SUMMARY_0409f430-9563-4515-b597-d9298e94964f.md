The theorem has been formalized and analyzed in `RequestProject/TapeReading.lean`. The analysis reveals that the claimed theorem is **partially true and partially false**:

### Claim 1 — Sufficient Statistic: ✅ TRUE (proved)

Under the Kyle model Y = λ·OFI + U (with U independent noise), the statistic T(X) = (λ·OFI, TFI·|OFI|) is indeed sufficient for Y. We prove this in three ways:

1. **`signal_eq_first_component_of_T`**: The signal λ·OFI is recoverable as the first component of T(X) — the algebraic core of sufficiency.

2. **`kyle_model_signal_determines_prediction`**: Under the model, Y decomposes as signal + noise where the signal depends on X only through λ·OFI.

3. **`condexp_signal_plus_noise`**: The measure-theoretic formulation: if Y = S + U where S is 𝒢-measurable and E[U|𝒢] = 0 a.e., then E[Y|𝒢] = S a.e. Since both σ(X) and σ(T) contain σ(S), we get E[Y|σ(X)] = S = E[Y|σ(T)], establishing that T captures all predictive information (I(Y; X | T(X)) = 0).

### Claim 2 — Classifier Bound: ❌ FALSE (disproved)

The claimed bound P(sign(Ŷ) = sign(Y)) ≥ 1/2 + λ·E[|x|]/(2·√(2π)·σ_u) is **false** as a universal bound. We prove this via two formal counterexample theorems:

- **`classifier_bound_false`**: With λ=1, E[|x|]=3, σ_u=1, the bound gives 1/2 + 3/(2√(2π)) > 1, which is impossible since probabilities are at most 1. The key inequality is 9 > 2π.

- **`claimed_bound_unbounded`**: The bound function 1/2 + z/(2√(2π)) is unbounded — for any M, there exists z ≥ 0 making the bound exceed M. A valid probability bound must stay ≤ 1.

### Corrected Bound (proved)

- **`gaussian_cdf_lower_bound`**: A valid universal lower bound on the Gaussian CDF: for z ≥ 0, Φ(z) ≥ 1/2 + z·exp(-z²/2)/√(2π). This gives a corrected classifier bound P(correct) ≥ 1/2 + E[|μ|·exp(-μ²/(2σ²))]/(√(2π)·σ) that naturally decays for large signal-to-noise ratios (unlike the original which diverges).
