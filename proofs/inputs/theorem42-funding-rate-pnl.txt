# Theorem 42: Funding Rate P&L Impact

## Definitions

Let d ∈ {+1, -1} be the position direction (+1 = long, -1 = short).
Let r_f(t) be the funding rate at settlement period t.
Let T be the number of settlement periods the position is held.

The funding P&L over T periods is:
  F = sum_{t=1}^T (-d * r_f(t))

For constant funding rate r_f:
  F = -d * r_f * T

Let alpha > 0 be the expected per-trade excess return (alpha).
Let H > 0 be the number of funding settlements per step.
Let T_bar be the average hold duration in steps.

## Claims

1. **Funding P&L formula.** For a long position (d = +1) with constant positive funding rate r_f > 0 held for T periods:
     F = -r_f * T < 0

   The long pays funding. For a short position (d = -1):
     F = r_f * T > 0

   The short receives funding. Prove that the sign of F is always opposite to d * r_f.

2. **Funding drag exceeds alpha condition.** The total funding cost over a trade exceeds the per-trade alpha when:
     |E[r_f]| * T_bar * H > alpha

   Rearranging, the maximum hold duration before funding exceeds alpha is:
     T_max = alpha / (|E[r_f]| * H)

3. **Numerical verification for our parameters.** With:
   - E[r_f] = 0.000012 (0.12 bps per hour, empirically measured)
   - H = 1/360 (approximately 1 funding settlement per 360 steps, since 1 hour ≈ 360 steps at ~10 sec/step)
   - T_bar = 1200 steps (our MIN_HOLD)
   - alpha = 0.008 (80 bps, our fee barrier)

   Compute:
     funding_cost_per_trade = |E[r_f]| * T_bar * H = 0.000012 * 1200 / 360

   Verify that funding_cost_per_trade < 0.00005 (less than 0.5 bps).
   Verify that funding_cost_per_trade / alpha < 0.01 (funding is less than 1% of the fee barrier).

4. **Asymmetric impact on longs vs shorts.** Given that funding is positive with probability p > 0.5 (empirically p ≈ 0.78):
   - Expected funding cost for a long position per settlement: E[r_f] > 0
   - Expected funding cost for a short position per settlement: -E[r_f] < 0 (shorts receive on average)

   For a strategy that is long with probability q and short with probability (1-q), the expected funding cost per settlement is:
     E[funding_cost] = q * E[r_f] - (1-q) * E[r_f] = (2q - 1) * E[r_f]

   Prove that a balanced strategy (q = 0.5) has zero expected funding cost, and only a long-biased strategy (q > 0.5) pays net funding.
